Research

Fallback supernovae of stripped stars with neutron star companions

For the first time, collisions between a neutron star (NS) and a black hole (BH) were detected earlier in 2021. Such collisions generate ripples in the fabric of spacetime that travel across the universe and can be detected on Earth using gravitational-wave observatories. These observations of NS-BH binaries, which have not been detected in the Milky Way yet, could mean that our ideas of how such systems are assembled might need to be revised.
In my project, I am studying a novel formation channel for such binary systems in which massive helium stars, formed after a common-envelope phase, remain compact and avoid mass transfer onto the NS companion, thereby evading pulsar recycling. In order to address this problem, I have been conducting three-dimensional Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics simulations of the supernova explosion of massive stripped helium stars using the GADGET-2 code. In particular, I have been focusing on the mass fallback evolution of the ejecta and the subsequent accretion onto the newly born NS.

I presented this work at the 2021 SACNAS NDiSTEM Digital Conference. You can find my poster here

Squeezed-coherent states

Squeezed states are widely used within quantum sensing and routinely improve the sensitivity of a range of different sensors, such as LIGO. Squeezed-coherent states are the most general, and they are created by applying the squeezing operator onto the ground state of the simple quantum harmonic oscillator, followed by applying the displacement operator. These states are increasingly becoming important across different subfields in physics and have been used in recent experiments as a way to improve stimulated emission microscopy for biological systems, and as a way to measure the small changes induced by axions in resonant-microwave-cavity-based searches for dark matter. Hence, my collaborators and I expect an increased interest in working with these states. In our work, we showed three different ways to construct the wavefunction for squeezed-coherent states: (i) a differential equation-based approach; (ii) an approach that uses an expansion in terms of the simple-harmonic oscillator wavefunctions; and (iii) a fully operator-based approach.

Online Sciathon 2020

In the summer of 2020, I participated in a hackathon-style online competition run by the council for the Lindau Nobel Laureate Meetings. My team (Group Elmiger) created a webinar platform to make research seminars more accesible to people around the world. This was an interdisciplinary project that was completed by a group of seven young scientists from seven countries working in different fields of science: economics, chemistry, and physics.
Soon after this competition ended, we discovered that a group of senior mathematicians had just started a similar project called researchseminars.org, and we decided to partner with them to work towards the same goal.
